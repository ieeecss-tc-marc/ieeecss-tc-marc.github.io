Scope

In an era defined by dynamic technological progress, the integration of manufacturing automation and the control of robotic systems stands at the forefront of innovation and development. The IEEE Control Systems Society's Technical Committee on Manufacturing Automation and Robotic Control (TC-MARC) represents a pivotal hub, bringing together a diverse community of researchers and professionals, all united by a common passion for advancing the realms of manufacturing automation and robotic control.

The present world confronts a multitude of complexities, spanning from scarcities in labor to disruptions in supply chains, coupled with a burgeoning demand for integrated circuits. Within this intricate backdrop, the significance of automation gains paramount importance, alongside the pivotal roles enacted by robots and autonomous systems. This confluence of factors acutely underscores the urgency of their roles in bolstering economic advancement and meeting the diverse array of societal needs. Recent global occurrences have served to underscore the myriad benefits inherent in the deployment and proficient management of autonomous and unmanned systems. It is by embracing the adoption of modular, collaborative, and economically efficient robot teams that we chart a disciplined course towards accomplishing our goals, thereby augmenting performance across a wide spectrum of applications. The intricacies inherent in the control of robotic systems necessitate the collaboration and integration of expertise spanning multiple disciplines and research domains.

The TC-MARC, a dynamic consortium comprising dedicated volunteers from academia, government, and industry, stands as a beacon of knowledge exchange and technical collaboration. Our primary mission revolves around the orchestration of forums and meetings, creating a fertile ground for in-depth technical discussions among researchers who share a profound interest in the domains of manufacturing automation, robotic system control, and an array of interrelated research topics. Our committee's pursuits extend far and wide, encompassing critical subjects such as autonomy, control and guidance of unmanned systems, human-robot interaction, pursuit-evasion games, and intelligent control. Through these diverse areas of focus, TC-MARC aims to advance the boundaries of knowledge and practical implementation, nurturing a community of scholars and professionals who are deeply committed to innovation and progress.
